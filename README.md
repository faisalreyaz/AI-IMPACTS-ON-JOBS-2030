# 🤖 Workforce 2030: AI Impacts on Jobs

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes how **Artificial Intelligence (AI) is expected to impact jobs by 2030**, focusing on automation risk, salary trends, and skill requirements across different roles.

Built entirely in **Power BI**, the dashboard provides an interactive and data-driven view of which jobs are most vulnerable, which are safest, and how factors like **education and experience influence automation probability**.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Identify jobs at **high risk of automation**
- Highlight **safe and future-proof careers**
- Analyze the relationship between **experience and automation probability**
- Compare **salary trends across education levels**
- Provide insights into **workforce transformation by 2030**

## 🔍 Key Insights

### ⚠️ Jobs by Risk Level
- Majority of roles fall under **Low to Medium risk**
- A smaller subset of jobs shows **high automation probability**

👉 Indicates partial automation rather than full job replacement  

---

### 🚨 Top 5 Risky Jobs
- Retail Worker  
- Security Guard  
- Construction Worker  
- Customer Support  
- Truck Driver  

👉 These roles involve **repetitive or routine tasks**, making them highly automatable  

---

### 🛡️ Top 5 Safest Jobs
- Doctor  
- Teacher  
- Nurse  
- Research Scientist  
- AI Engineer  

👉 Jobs requiring **human judgment, creativity, and expertise** are least likely to be automated  

---

### 💰 Salary by Education
- Master’s Degree — Highest average salary (~91K)  
- PhD — ~89K  
- Bachelor’s — ~89K  
- High School — Lowest (~88K)  

👉 Higher education correlates with **better pay and lower automation risk**  

---

### 📊 Experience vs Automation
- Automation probability remains **relatively consistent across experience levels**
- Experience alone does not significantly reduce automation risk  

👉 Skill type matters more than years of experience  

---

## 🧠 Key Takeaway
> The future workforce will not be defined by experience alone, but by adaptability, advanced skills, and education.  
>  
> While AI will automate repetitive tasks, it will also increase demand for roles that require **critical thinking, domain expertise, and human interaction**.
